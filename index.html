Child Development Milestones (3–4 Years)

Track Aria’s progress in these key developmental areas. The list includes both expected and advanced milestones.

Gross Motor Skills

  • Runs smoothly and changes direction easily
  • Jumps off both feet and lands steadily
  • Climbs stairs with alternating feet
  • Rides a tricycle confidently
  • Balances on one foot for several seconds
  • Skips or gallops with good rhythm
  • Hops continuously on one foot for 10 seconds
  • Throws and catches a large ball with accuracy

Fine Motor Skills

  • Draws basic shapes (circles and squares)
  • Uses child-safe scissors (with supervision)
  • Manipulates small objects (blocks, puzzles)
  • Draws a person with at least 3 body parts
  • Copies simple letters or numbers
  • Cuts out simple shapes from paper

Language & Communication

  • Speaks in full sentences
  • Follows multi-step instructions
  • Uses a wide vocabulary in conversation
  • Tells a detailed story with a clear beginning, middle, and end
  • Asks various “why” and “how” questions
  • Uses descriptive language (colors, sizes, emotions)

Social & Emotional Development

  • Plays cooperatively with peers
  • Expresses basic emotions appropriately
  • Takes turns and listens to others
  • Demonstrates empathy and offers comfort to a friend
  • Resolves minor conflicts verbally
  • Shows leadership or initiative in group play

Cognitive Development

  • Recognises basic colors and shapes
  • Counts to 10 or higher
  • Solves simple puzzles
  • Sorts objects by multiple attributes (e.g., color and shape)
  • Understands basic time concepts (today, tomorrow, yesterday)
  • Retells events in sequence using memory

Parenting Advice & Goals

Use these interactive tools and reminders to support your parenting journey:

Establish Routines

  • Set a consistent daily schedule (meals, play, bedtime)
  • Involve Aria in choosing between limited options

Positive Discipline

  • Praise specific good behaviors
  • Explain boundaries and natural consequences

Engagement & Learning

  • Read together daily and ask open-ended questions
  • Encourage imaginative play and problem solving

Self-Care & Reflection

  • Schedule regular "me-time" for self-care
  • Reflect on parenting challenges and adjust as needed
